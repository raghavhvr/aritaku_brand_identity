01 — The Name

Why Aritaku.

Aritaku (అరిటాకు) is Telugu for banana leaf — the original plate of South India. Every person from Warangal has eaten from one. No word is more honest, more local, or more deeply tied to the act of being fed with love. It's two syllables. It's unmistakable. It's ours.

01

Deeply Local

Every Telugu person knows this word from childhood — festivals, weddings, Sunday meals at amma's house. It carries no pretense. Zero borrowed identity. 100% Warangal.

02

Honest by Nature

A banana leaf has no hiding place — the food is right there, visible, pure. It perfectly mirrors the open kitchen and no-stale-food promise of the brand.

03

Unforgettable

Two syllables. Easy to say, easy to spell. "Let's go to Aritaku" — it rolls off the tongue. It sticks. No one else has claimed it as a brand in F&B.

02 — Brand Strategy

The Business of a Morning.

Aritaku is not competing with restaurants. It is claiming the morning — the 6am–10am ritual that Warangal has never had a proper home for. This is the strategic frame.

V

Vision

To become Warangal's most recognised breakfast identity — the brand name people say when they mean "great tiffin." Within 3 years, a destination. Within 5, a franchise-ready format.

M

Mission

Serve Warangal's most honest breakfast every morning. No frozen, no stale, no shortcuts. Open kitchen. Ground-fresh chutneys. Cold-pressed oil. The banana leaf as the only plate we need.

P

Positioning

The Premium Traditional Tiffin Centre. Not a hotel. Not a fast food joint. The place that earns the same loyalty as amma's kitchen — by doing the same things amma did, just every morning for everyone.

A

Audience

Primary: Working adults 25–45 in Warangal who remember what real tiffin tastes like. Secondary: Students and young families discovering it for the first time. Tertiary: Diaspora visitors — NRIs, relatives, anyone who misses the taste.

07 — Brand Voice

How Aritaku speaks.

Aritaku speaks like the person behind the counter who knows your name. Warm, direct, never trying too hard. Telugu pride runs underneath every word, whether in English or the mother tongue.

Warm & Direct
We speak like people who have been doing this for generations. Short sentences. No fluff. No corporate speak. Like amma, not like a brand manager.
Say this
"Fresh batch of pesarattu. Come fast."
Not this
"Introducing our curated selection of authentic handcrafted breakfast options."
Locally Rooted
Telugu words appear naturally — not as exoticism, not as translation. They belong here. We don't over-explain them. The audience knows.
Say this
"Upma ready. Side: kobbari chutney, today's batch."
Not this
"Our famous Semolina Porridge with authentic coconut condiment."
Quietly Proud
Never boastful, never begging for validation. State the facts — the quality speaks for itself. Pride is in the doing, not in saying you're the best.
Say this
"Same groundnut oil we've always used. Same chutney stone."
Not this
"Warangal's #1 best authentic tiffin experience! 5-star rated!"

08 — Brand Do's & Don'ts

The lines we hold.

✓ Do this

✓

Use both languages naturally

Telugu and English co-exist. Switch between them organically. "Today's special: Punukulu · పునుకులు" feels right.

✓

Show the process

Film the grinding, the tawa, the hands pressing the dosa. The kitchen is the brand. Show it freely.

✓

Own the banana leaf

The leaf is always the hero of every photo, every composition. Everything served on it, presented on it, photographed on it.

✓

Be specific about freshness

"Ground at 5:30am" is better than "fresh." Times, batches, sourcing details — specificity = credibility.

✓

Keep colour palette strict

Only use the 12 approved brand colours. Especially on digital — no random gradients, no neon, no generic blue.

✗ Avoid this

✗

Generic "food" photography

Overhead shots with tweezers and microgreens. That's not who we are. Keep it real, warm, morning-light.

✗

Overly decorative fonts

No calligraphy scripts other than Libre Baskerville. No random Google Fonts added to materials. Type system is locked.

✗

English-only communication

We are a Telugu brand. A post in only English with zero Telugu feels inauthentic. Always include at least a Telugu phrase.

✗

Discount-first marketing

Do not lead with price cuts or "FREE with every order" messaging. It cheapens the brand. Quality leads. Value is felt, not discounted.

✗

Stretch or distort the logo

Never alter the proportions, add drop shadows, change the font, or use colours outside the approved set for the wordmark.

14 — Launch Roadmap

How Aritaku enters Warangal.

A phased approach. Build anticipation before you open. Let the brand be known before the first dosa is served. Every phase has a clear job.

01
Phase 1 · Weeks 1–3 · Pre-Launch

The Brand Arrives

Put up the exterior board. No "coming soon" — just the wordmark. Blank. Confident. Let Warangal wonder. Activate @aritaku.warangal with a single black post: just the logo. No captions yet. Create intrigue before explanation.

02
Phase 2 · Weeks 4–5 · Tease

The Kitchen Wakes Up

Post process content: the chutney stone, the batter bowl at 5am, hands laying banana leaves. No product shots yet. Just the ritual. Caption in Telugu only: "తెల్లవారుతోంది." (The morning is breaking.) Build mystique and local pride before the doors open.

03
Phase 3 · Day 1 · Launch

The Leaf is Set

Open at 6am. First 100 customers get a loyalty card. Film the first banana leaf being laid. Post live. No offer, no discount — just "We're open. Warangal, come eat." WhatsApp broadcast to all contacts. One poster on every auto and wall in the locality.

04
Phase 4 · Weeks 6–12 · Establishment

Becoming the Regular

Daily morning posts. Weekly featured item. Introduce "regulars" by name on social (with permission). Partner with 2–3 local auto/cab drivers to recommend Aritaku. Push Google Maps reviews. Build WhatsApp broadcast list to 500+.

05
Phase 5 · Month 4+ · Growth

Deepening Roots

Introduce weekend specials tied to Telugu festivals. Launch corporate tiffin delivery for offices within 2km. Begin franchise documentation if footfall exceeds targets. Commission a local artist for a wall mural featuring banana leaf and Warangal heritage motifs.
